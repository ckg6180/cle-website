GBP Basics

What Is Google Business Profile and Why It Matters for Local Service Businesses

Chris Gallagher March 22, 2026 6 min read

If you run a local service business — pressure washing, plumbing, HVAC, roofing, window cleaning — there's one free tool that has more impact on whether your phone rings than almost anything else you can do online.

It's called Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business), and it's the listing that shows up when someone searches for your type of service on Google Maps or types something like "plumber near me" into Google.

Here's the problem: most local service businesses either don't have one, barely filled it out, or set it up years ago and never touched it again. And that's costing them thousands of dollars in calls every single month.

What Exactly Is Google Business Profile?

Google Business Profile (GBP) is a free listing that Google provides to local businesses. It's what powers your appearance in:

  • Google Maps — when customers search for services in their area
  • The Local Pack — the top 3 map results that show up on the first page of Google search results
  • Google Search — the knowledge panel that appears on the right side when someone searches your business name

Your GBP listing includes your business name, address, phone number, hours, photos, reviews, services, and more. Think of it as your digital storefront on Google — except most of your customers see it before they ever visit your website.

Why Does It Matter So Much for Local Service Businesses?

Here's the reality that most business owners don't realize: the majority of local customers never make it to your website. They find you on Google Maps, look at your reviews, check your hours, and call you directly from your GBP listing.

Consider these facts:

  1. 46% of all Google searches have local intent. That means nearly half the people using Google are looking for something nearby — like your service.
  2. 76% of people who search for something nearby visit a business within 24 hours. These are high-intent customers ready to buy.
  3. The top 3 results in the Google Maps Local Pack get the vast majority of clicks and calls. If you're not in that top 3, you're essentially invisible.

For a local service business, your Google Business Profile isn't just another online listing. It's your #1 source of new customer calls — if it's set up and optimized correctly.

What Most Local Service Businesses Get Wrong

After optimizing Google Business Profiles for local service businesses, I see the same mistakes over and over:

1. Incomplete Profiles

Half-filled profiles signal to Google that you're not a serious, active business. Every empty field is a missed opportunity to rank higher. Google rewards completeness — businesses with complete profiles are 2.7x more likely to be considered reputable.

2. Wrong or Missing Categories

Your primary category tells Google what you do. If it's wrong or too generic, you won't show up for the right searches. Many businesses also miss the opportunity to add secondary categories that capture additional search traffic.

3. No Review Strategy

Reviews are the single biggest ranking factor for the Local Pack. Yet most businesses leave this to chance. You need a system — a repeatable process for asking happy customers to leave a review at the right moment.

4. Stale Content

Google wants to see that your business is active. If your last photo was uploaded two years ago and you've never posted a Google Post, Google has no reason to prioritize you over a competitor who's posting weekly.

5. No Photos (or Bad Ones)

Businesses with photos receive 42% more requests for driving directions and 35% more click-throughs to their websites. Yet most service businesses have zero photos or a handful of blurry phone pics from 2019.

Key Takeaway

Your Google Business Profile is likely the first thing potential customers see when they search for your service. An incomplete, outdated, or unoptimized profile isn't just a missed opportunity — it's actively sending customers to your competitors.

What a Fully Optimized GBP Looks Like

A Google Business Profile that's working hard for your business has:

  • Accurate, complete business information — name, address, phone, hours, service area, all matching exactly across the web
  • The right primary and secondary categories — telling Google exactly what searches to show you for
  • A keyword-rich business description — naturally incorporating the services and locations you want to rank for
  • Fresh, high-quality photos — updated regularly, showing your work, your team, and your results
  • Consistent Google Posts — weekly updates showing Google (and customers) that you're active
  • A steady stream of reviews — with thoughtful owner responses to every single one
  • Complete Q&A section — proactively answering common customer questions
  • Accurate service listings — with descriptions that match what customers are searching for

When all of these elements are working together, Google has every reason to rank you in the top 3 for local searches in your area. And that's when your phone starts ringing more.

The Bottom Line

If you're a local service business and you're not actively optimizing your Google Business Profile, you're leaving money on the table every single day. Your competitors who are doing this — even if they're newer, smaller, or less experienced than you — will outrank you and get the calls that should be going to your business.
